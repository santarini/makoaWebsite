Monte Carlo Simulation

Computational algorithms that perform repeated random sampling to create probability models

Monte Carlo simulations are used to model the probability of different outcomes in a process that cannot easily be predicted due to the intervention of random variables. They can be used to understand the impact of risk and uncertainty in prediction and forecasting models. Their essential idea is using randomness to solve problems that might be deterministic in principle.

On my GitHub I have uploaded Python functions that simulate Coin Flips, Dice Rolls, and Stock Market fluctuations. The output resulting from these programs can be plotted using MatPlotLib or exported to CSV to be used by Excel or SPSS.
